## Common Investor Questions & Answers

**Q: Why not just build software? Why hardware?**
A: Hardware creates physical presence in schools, stronger lock-in, and enables offline use (important in some markets). But we validate software first to reduce risk.

**Q: What about ChatGPT/Claude? Can't they do this?**
A: Generic LLMs aren't optimized for K-12 homework, lack school integration, and don't provide teacher workflow. We're a vertical solution, not a horizontal tool.

**Q: How will you acquire customers?**
A: Teacher referrals (viral), direct sales to schools, partnerships with tutoring centers. B2B2C model lowers friction.

**Q: What if teachers resist AI?**
A: We position as assistant, not replacement. Pilot teachers show high interest when positioned as "time-saving tool" not "teaching replacement."

**Q: Regulatory risks?**
A: We augment teachers, not replace them. Data privacy is core design. Compliance built-in from Day 1.

**Q: Why $1M? Can you do it for less?**
A: We could do MVP for $500K, but that leaves no room for hardware phase or market traction. $1M gives us 18 months to reach profitability.

**Q: What's your exit strategy?**
A: Strategic acquisition by EdTech platform (Google, Microsoft, Canvas) or IPO if we reach scale. $500M-$1B valuation in 5 years is realistic if we execute.
